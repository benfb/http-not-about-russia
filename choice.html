Alya:

Since the beginning of class we have been told that there is no choice.

Hadji Murat was doomed from the start; the thistle was plucked in the first words.

Anna died the moment she met eyes with Vronsky.

Shklovsky believed "the beginning was already played out," that
no one can change the denouement," that "tragic endings—at the very least,
a broken heart—are inevitable in an epistolary novel."

Now, reading Dostoevsky, we learn that there is choice. We are all allowed a suddenly.
